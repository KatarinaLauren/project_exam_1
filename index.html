A SpaceX timeline

1971

Elon Musk is born

1971

Elon Musk

  • Was born 28 June 1971 in Pretoria, South Africa
  • Became a multi millionaire in his late 20s
  • 1999 Founder of X.com - later PayPal
  • 2002 Founder and CEO of SpaceX
  • 2003 Co-founder and CEO of Tesla Motors
  • One of the most well-known people on the planet today

2002

SpaceX is founded

2002

Space Exploration Technologies Corp

The ultimate goal of SpaceX is reducing space transportation costs to eventually enable the colonization of Mars.

Elon Musk calculated that the material cost of building a rocket at the time was about 3% of the actual sales price of a rocket. He saw huge potential to lower this cost and still make a good profit.

On 6th of May 2002, SpaceX was founded.

SpaceX homepage

2005

Falcon 1

2005

Development and testing of Falcon 1

SpaceX begins testing Falcon 1, its first real rocket design created with the goal of reusable space flight.

November 25th 2005 the first Falcon 1 launch is attempted.

The first launch failed

SpaceX launches SpaceX rockets

2006-2007

Falcon 1 failures

2006-2007

SpaceX continues to test and try and launch the Falcon 1 rocket. All attempted launches fail.

Despite the failures, SpaceX still manages to win a NASA contract and secure additional funding for the company.

In 2007 SpaceX also moves to Hawthorne, California, where they are still located today.

2008

First launch success

2008

The first successful launch of Falcon 1!

After the third Falcon 1 failure in August 2008 where 3 satellites where lost, the future of SpaceX was looking less bright and they were in need of a win.

On 28th of September 2008 a milestone mission was carried out.

The fourth launch of Falcon 1 was a success.

Falcon 1 becomes the first privately-developed liquid-fuel launch vehicle to go into orbit around the Earth.

2009

First satellite delivered

2009

First satellite delivered to Earth orbit

On 14th of July 2009 Falcon 1 has its fifth flight and becomes the first privately developed liquid fuel rocket to deliver a commercial satellite to Earth orbit.

in 2009 Elon Musk also joins Twitter, where he becomes a notable figure.
